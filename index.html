  • 6th century BC: The Edomites establish a settlement in the area that will become Petra.
  • th century BC: The Nabataeans take control of Petra and begin to develop it into a major trading hub.
  • 312 BC: The Seleucid Empire takes control of Petra.
  • 312-301 BC: Petra comes under the control of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt.
  • 168 BC: The Nabataeans regain control of Petra.
  • 9 BC - 40 AD: The reign of King Aretas IV, during which Petra reaches its peak of prosperity and prestige.
  • 106 AD: Petra is annexed by the Roman Empire under Emperor Trajan.
  • 363 AD: Petra is hit by a massive earthquake, causing extensive damage to many of its buildings.
  • 7th century: The rise of Islam leads to Petra losing its strategic importance and being gradually abandoned.
  • 1812: Swiss explorer Johann Ludwig Burckhardt rediscovers Petra.
  • 1929: The Transjordan Frontier Force captures Petra from a local bandit group.
  • 1985: Petra is designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Present day: Petra remains a popular tourist destination and a symbol of Jordan's rich cultural heritage.
